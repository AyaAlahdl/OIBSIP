Space

Galaxies

Galaxies are vast systems of stars, planets, gas, and dust held together by gravity. The Milky Way, our home galaxy, contains billions of stars. The Hubble Space Telescope has revealed breathtaking images of distant galaxies, showcasing their diverse shapes and colors.

Nebulas

Nebulas are colossal clouds of gas and dust in space. They can be nurseries for new stars or remnants of exploded stars. Nebulas come in various forms, such as the iconic Eagle Nebula's Pillars of Creation or the vibrant colors of the Orion Nebula.

Black Holes

Black holes are regions in space where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape their pull. They are formed from the remnants of massive stars that have collapsed under their own gravity. While they are invisible, their presence can be inferred by their effects on surrounding matter and light.

Exoplanets

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside of our solar system. The discovery of exoplanets has expanded our understanding of planetary systems and the possibility of finding habitable worlds beyond Earth. Some exoplanets may have the potential for hosting life or possess unique characteristics.
